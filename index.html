The North Star

This is a five-month experiment in building a life on your own terms. You've spent years being excellent at a job that asks a lot of you — and you've given it. Now you're asking a different question: What would it look like if I directed some of that excellence toward myself?


The answer isn't grand or sudden. It looks like: taking Fridays off and actually resting. Following a lead that came in today about spiritual leadership. Showing up on a job site with your GC and learning to read a set of plans. Having a real conversation with Ross about what you both want. Letting a work email wait until tomorrow.


A word about privilege: yes, you have it. Having more options than most people is real, and you're right not to be glib about it. But here's the thing — shrinking yourself doesn't close that gap. Staying in a life that doesn't fit you out of guilt doesn't help anyone who has fewer choices. What might actually matter is what you do with the freedom: build something, guide someone, have a conversation that creates more light in the world. That's the direction to run in.


By August, you want to be able to say: I know more clearly what I'm building toward. I've started building it. And I proved I can live on less and feel like more.

I am finding its right size."

Active ▼
Intent

Key Milestones
  • Apr–May
    Draft your personal definition of "appropriate ownership" — what does it mean to be good at this job without giving it your whole self?
  • WFH Days

  • Late May
    ⭐ Initiate conversation with boss about extending reduced schedule through year-end
  • June

  • Ongoing
    Monthly: write one paragraph naming what you're choosing not to own this month at work, and why that's okay

And if the guilt shows up: Did I confuse self-punishment with fairness? Overworking doesn't redistribute anything. Using this time well might.

Financial Intentionality

Ongoing ▼
Intent
Prove to yourself that you can live on less and feel okay — even good. Build the runway that makes every other pivot feel possible rather than terrifying. This is about freedom, not deprivation.
Key Milestones
  • Apr
    ⭐ Spend audit: identify and cut optional recurring expenses (extra fitness classes, monthly massage, etc.)
  • Apr
    Set a monthly spending target — not punishing, but intentional
  • Apr+
    Resourcefulness practice: before buying anything for a project, check what you already have first
  • Monthly
    Review last month's spending — celebrate wins, adjust without shame
  • Aug
    ⭐ Look back at 5 months of data: do you feel more financially confident? What would it now feel like to make less?
Weekly Check-in Prompt
Ask yourself: Did I spend in line with my values this week? Did I use what I have before buying something new? What optional thing did I let go of — and how did that feel?

And if the guilt shows up: Having more than others is real. But spending carelessly doesn't help them, and building financial steadiness gives you real choices — including choices that create space to contribute more.

The Season's Invitation

Spring is the season of emergence — of things that have been quietly growing underground finally deciding to push through. You don't force spring. You create conditions for it and then you trust it.


That's what this season is. You're not manufacturing a transformation. You're creating the conditions: a little more space, a little less weight, a few honest conversations, some sawdust on your boots. And then you trust.


One more thing worth naming: you've been carrying the awareness of your privilege like a tax on your own joy. That awareness is good — it means you're paying attention. But there's a difference between awareness and penance. The goal isn't to suffer enough to feel like you've earned the right to rest. The goal is to use the rest, the freedom, the options — to build something real. To show up more fully for the people and work that matter. To become more yourself, not less.


You already know what you're becoming. You're just giving it permission.
